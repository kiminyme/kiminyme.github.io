About the Course About the Instructor Course Requirements

This course is designed to provide an overview of general topics related to the use of personal computers.

Topics include:

  • What is it? How computers are used today.
  • Getting it together. How networks and the Internet work.
  • Taking it apart. What do all the parts do?
  • Taking control! How software runs the computer.

Weekly assignments include a short (1-2 page) research paper or essay, interactive discussions with other students in the class, and readings from a variety of sources.

There will also be bi-weekly projects, a midterm exam, and a final exam.

Kim Weiss has been a full-time instructor in the College of Information and Computer Technology since 2001.

She has a Masters degree from Indiana University, and she is currently pursuing a Masters in Educational Technology through Boise State University.

She is A+ certified, and holds Expert certification in several versions of Microsoft Office.
