Copyright and Plagiarism

This activity is designed for a first-year university level course in computer concepts. The goal is to be able to explain what actions constitute plagiarism, how to avoid plagiarism, and what copyright entails, as well as to demonstrate appropriate use of citations and references in a research project.

In this assignment, you will use the World Wide Web to find answers to specific questions, and complete a worksheet to be submitted to the instructor.

Click here to download a copy of the worksheet. You can print out a copy and write out the answers by hand, or open the worksheet on the computer and enter the information in the spaces provided.

Part 1: Plagiarism

Go to the Website plagiarism.org. Use the pages on that site to answer the following questions.

  • Click the link entitled Plagiarism 101, then open the link for “What is Plagiarism?" List three of the actions they list as acts of plagiarism from that site.
  • Click the link entitled Types of Plagiarism in the left-hand pane. Give the number and name of THREE of the examples given on that page, along with the description the page gives for each type.
  • Click the link at the top of the page that reads Citing Sources, then click the link for What’s a Citation? Use the information in that page to answer the following questions.
    • What are the FIVE things you should include in a complete citation or reference whenever possible?
    • List the five situations when you are normally expected to cite sources when writing a paper.

Part 2: U.S. Copyright Law

This part of the activity will help you understand U.S. Copyright Law, as well as give you practice using citations to indicate the sources you used to find the information.

Use a search engine like Bing or Google to find pages that answer the following questions. As you find the answers, provide complete reference information for each source using the space provided in the worksheet.

You may find one page that provides answers to all of the questions, or you may find different pages. For each question, though, you must provide information about the source used for each answer, even if all answers come from the same source.

  • List at least three different kinds of work that are protected by U.S. Copyright Law.
  • How does the creator of one of these types of work obtain protection under copyright law?
  • When does copyright expire for each of the following types of creators:
    • The work was created by a single, known author or artist.
    • The work is anonymous.
    • The work was generated by a corporation, on a "for hire" basis.
  • List three things that cannot be protected by copyright?
  • What is "academic fair use?"
  • Give at least three examples of "fair use" that are NOT related to being a student?
  • What does the term "public domain" mean in reference to copyright?

Provide References

At the end of the worksheet, you should provide a list of sources in APA format. Use the examples below to make sure the references are presented correctly.

For most references:

Smith, M. (2013, January 1) Title of web page. Retrieved from http://completeurl.com/woiheongang/oagnaiengeng.html

If there is no author information available:

Title of web page. (2013, January 1) Retrieved from http://completeurl.com/woiheongang/oagnaiengeng.html

If there is no date available:

Smith, M. (n.d.) Title of web page. Retrieved from http://completeurl.com/woiheongang/oagnaiengeng.html

If there is no author or date information available:

Title of web page. (n.d.) Retrieved from http://completeurl.com/woiheongang/oagnaiengeng.html

The best way to get a complete URL for a Web page is to copy the address from the address box of the browser window while you are reading the page. Click here to open a video that shows how to do this using Windows.

If you need help understanding what information should be included in a citation or complete reference using APA standards, consult the library's guide at http://libguides.sullivan.edu/apa. You are also welcome to contact your instructor, a tutor in the tutoring center, or a librarian.
