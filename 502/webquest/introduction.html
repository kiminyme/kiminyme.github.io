Introduction

Now that you have learned what the different parts of a computer do, it's time to use that information to put together the perfect computer.

Background

Buying a new computer should be done in stages, whether you are buying a desktop computer, a notebook, a tablet, or a smart phone. If you go into a computer store, you are likely to be overwhelmed with the choices, and it is common for new computer users to make the mistake of buying a computer based on price and/or a salesperson's recommendation.

In reality, you want to look carefully first at what you want the new device to do, and identify the features that you need in a new system ahead of time. Then, when you walk into a computer showroom, you can make more intelligent decisions in your purchase. This Web quest will walk you through that process.

There are several steps to this exercies:

  • Introduction: This page gives you an overview of the exercise.
  • Task: This page details the steps you should complete in the course of the exercise.
  • Process: This page will guide you through completing each of the tasks.
  • Evaluation: You will perform a self-evaluation of how well you completed the tasks.
  • Conclusion: This will be your explanation of the steps you took and why you reached the conclusions you reached.
